Flash Flood Awareness

What is a Flash Flood?

A flash flood is a rapid flooding of low-lying areas, usually caused by heavy rainfall in a short period of time. They can occur with little to no warning and can be extremely dangerous.

How to Stay Safe

  • Stay informed: Listen to weather alerts and advisories.
  • Avoid flood-prone areas: Do not walk or drive through floodwaters.
  • Have an emergency kit ready: Include essentials like water, food, flashlight, and a first aid kit.
  • Have an evacuation plan: Know how to quickly reach higher ground if necessary.
  • Do not camp near rivers or streams during heavy rain.

What to Do During a Flash Flood

If you are caught in a flash flood:

  1. Move to higher ground immediately.
  2. Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.
  3. Follow evacuation orders if issued.
  4. Stay tuned to local news and weather updates.
