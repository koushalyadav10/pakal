Coastal Flood Awareness

What is a Coastal Flood?

A coastal flood is the inundation of land areas along the coast by seawater. This type of flooding is often caused by storm surges during severe weather events such as hurricanes or nor'easters.

How to Stay Safe

  • Stay informed: Monitor weather forecasts and warnings from local authorities.
  • Know your evacuation routes and have a plan in place.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, medications, and important documents.
  • Protect your property: Elevate utilities, install flood barriers, and secure outdoor items.
  • During a coastal flood watch or warning, follow instructions from emergency officials and evacuate if necessary.

What to Do During a Coastal Flood

If you are in an area affected by a coastal flood:

  1. Evacuate to higher ground immediately if instructed to do so.
  2. Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.
  3. Stay away from beaches, piers, and other coastal areas.
  4. Stay tuned to local news and weather updates for the latest information.
  5. Check on neighbors, especially those who may need assistance.
