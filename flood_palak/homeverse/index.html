    A river flood refers to the overflow of water from a river or stream onto adjacent land areas, typically caused by heavy rainfall, rapid snowmelt, ice jams, or a combination of these factors. This inundation of water can result in the temporary submersion of nearby homes, businesses, infrastructure, and agricultural land.

    A coastal flood is a type of flooding event that occurs when water from the ocean or other bodies of water inundates low-lying coastal areas, often as a result of storm surges, or extreme weather events.
